My background

I am a geologist and have a master's in Computer Science, currently working as a research geologist on a project of the Department of Computer Science of the UFMG with Petrobras.

I'm a PhD candidate in Earth and Planetary Sciences at McGill University, Canada

I've completed a Bachelor's in Geology and a master's program in Computer Science at the Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG), one of the top five universities in Brazil. I have worked as a research geologist on a project of the Department of Computer Science of the UFMG with Petrobras (Brazil's state-owned oil company) to develop new machine-learning methods applied to geological processing for oil-exploration applications. This project allows me to work with the interpretation of geophysical surveys, such as well-log/petrophysical and seismic data, mainly focused on processing and analyzing large volumes of data for automated interpretation.

I have previously worked as a geologist for a consulting company, conducting fieldwork and creating diverse geological and geotechnical models and reports used for risk analysis in mining pits utilizing Python, QGIS, and Leapfrog software. I have a strong facility for learning new ones.

Despite the recent computational approach, I have a strong foundation in geology, petrology, structural geology, engineering geology. Beyond my current work and Python studies, I have attended several courses in Data Analysis and Data Science, Numerical Modeling, SQL, and Statistics.

I am very enthusiastic about language studies, with English proficiency (IELTS 7.5 overall grade, C1) and advanced knowledge of German, having studied in German at the TU Bergakademie Freiberg. Also, I am currently learning French.
